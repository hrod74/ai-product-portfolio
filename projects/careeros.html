CareerOS: AI-Assisted Product Workflow System

A self-built operating system for turning a complex career search into structured decision support, reusable workflows, and human-reviewed application materials.

CareerOS is a self-built, AI-assisted operating system for managing complex career workflows. It centralizes the parts of a serious job search that normally live in scattered documents, browser tabs, and memory: role evaluation, resume tailoring, outreach planning, interview preparation, and post-outcome learning.

The system is intentionally designed as a structured, repeatable product rather than ad hoc job-search activity. It has a single source of truth, a library of named workflows, explicit decision frameworks, and human review built into every step that produces an external artifact.

Problem

Fragmented information makes career decisions slower and less consistent.

Career exploration creates fragmented information. Resumes, job descriptions, research notes, networking activity, application records, and interview prep tend to live in separate places and drift out of sync. Context gets recreated from scratch on every new opportunity, which is slow and inconsistent.

Most job-search tools are passive trackers. They record what happened, but they do not help evaluate an opportunity, preserve reasoning, tailor materials, or learn from outcomes. They store state without supporting decisions.

The challenge was to design a system that could evaluate opportunities against a consistent rubric, preserve context between sessions, tailor materials quickly without inventing facts, and learn from outcomes while keeping the human in control of every meaningful decision.

Product Goals

A repeatable system for quality, speed, and judgment.

Create a single source of truth for profile, accomplishments, role criteria, and voice.
Build repeatable workflows for role evaluation, resume tailoring, outreach, interview prep, and post-outcome learning.
Use AI to accelerate research, synthesis, and drafting without inventing facts, metrics, or tools.
Improve prioritization by separating role fit from screen-pass probability.
Keep sensitive decisions human-reviewed before anything external is sent.
Reduce repetitive work while improving consistency and quality.

System Architecture

Designed as a product system, not a pile of prompts.

Profile source of truth

Canonical files hold the master career profile, accomplishment bank, target-role rubric, and voice guide.

Job tracker structure

A documented schema defines opportunities, stages, statuses, and reporting fields. The system recommends updates, but a human applies them.

Workflow library

Named, versioned workflows define purpose, inputs, outputs, human review points, and guardrails.

Role research files

Per-opportunity research, fit assessments, and decision notes are separated into active and archived sets.

Application materials

Markdown review drafts come before final documents, with approval gates before anything external.

Interview prep

Role-specific prep briefs and post-interview debriefs are anchored to documented experience and the specific role.

Session notes and operating instructions

A running log and instruction file preserve system rules, decisions, and operating conventions across sessions.

Workflow Library

Named workflows make the system reusable.

Job Fit Review

Evaluates opportunities before tailoring by producing a fit score, top strengths, top gaps, role-lane classification, screen-pass read, and one recommended action.

Resume Tailoring

Adapts the master resume to a specific role using documented accomplishments and a markdown-first review process. The master resume is never modified.

Outreach

Creates short, targeted, human-sounding messages built around one genuine hook and one clear ask.

Weekly Review

Keeps the pipeline honest by surfacing stale items, pipeline status, and a small set of concrete next actions.

Networking Routine

Supports consistent targeted networking without making outreach a blocker on every application.

Interview Prep

Produces role-specific prep briefs with mapped stories, likely questions, talking points, questions to ask, and final checklist items.

Loss Review and Feedback Loop

Turns rejections into calibration by identifying funnel stage, likely cause, what worked, what to tighten, and lessons to carry forward.

Cover Letter Research and Drafting

Produces grounded research and markdown drafts before final documents, with explicit source and accuracy checks.

How I Used AI

AI is used for synthesis and speed, not blind automation.

Structured inspection

AI agents and coding assistants inspect structured files, compare role requirements against documented experience, and draft markdown review files.

Research and drafting

AI accelerates research, synthesis, and drafting inside a system where human judgment still owns positioning and approval.

Required human review

Human review is required before resumes, cover letters, or outreach go external.

Manual change guides

When document generation became unreliable, the system shifted to precise manual change guides instead of forcing fragile automation.

Accuracy guardrails

Guardrails block invented metrics, overstated claims, unused tools, and misrepresentation of shipped versus prototyped work.

Decision Frameworks

The system supports decisions without making them for the human.

Fit scoring

Opportunities are scored against level, domain, tooling, culture signals, and compensation fit.

Application effort levels

Strategic opportunities receive more investment than low-yield applications.

Role lanes

Opportunities are classified into lanes such as core senior product, applied AI, contract or bridge, and adjacent.

Capability fit versus screen-pass probability

The system separately scores “Can I do this job?” and “Will this application get a human read?”

Single recommended action

Each fit review ends with one clear action such as apply now, apply plus targeted outreach, recruiter path preferred, network first only, or hold or skip.

Outcome-first bullet rule

Resume bullets lead with measurable results only when real metrics exist.

Source-of-truth validation

Downstream materials are checked against canonical profile files to keep titles, tools, metrics, and ownership language accurate.

Iterations and Key Improvements

The system matured through repeated use and calibration.

  1. Started as a structured profile and tracker schema.
  2. Added role-fit workflows so opportunities were evaluated consistently.
  3. Added resume tailoring and cover letter workflows with explicit voice and accuracy rules.
  4. Shifted to markdown review drafts before final documents.
  5. Added manual change guides when automated document formatting proved unreliable.
  6. Added a separate screen-pass probability score after observing that strong capability fit does not always pass automated screens.
  7. Refined networking so it stayed selective and useful rather than mandatory for every application.
  8. Added privacy and accuracy guardrails throughout.

Results and Impact

Measured in better process, consistency, and judgment.

  • Reduced repeated context setting by maintaining a single source of truth.
  • Improved consistency of role evaluations through a shared rubric and explicit lanes.
  • Created faster resume tailoring through a markdown-first, review-then-build process.
  • Improved prioritization by separating capability fit from screen-pass probability.
  • Preserved a reusable career knowledge base that compounds over time.
  • Created a framework for evaluating AI-assisted product workflows, including where to automate and where to keep a human in the loop.
  • Demonstrated applied AI fluency through a working system, not just a list of tools.

  • More automated status reconciliation with the tracker, while keeping final updates human-applied.
  • Stronger privacy redaction for portfolio exports.
  • A reusable interview story library mapped to common question types.
  • Role-specific score trend analysis over time.
