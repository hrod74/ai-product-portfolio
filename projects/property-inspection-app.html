Property Inspection App Prototype

An AI-assisted mobile prototype built for an interview presentation to demonstrate product thinking, workflow design, and rapid execution.

I built a mobile property inspection app prototype as part of an interview process to demonstrate how quickly I could turn a product concept into a working, reviewable experience using modern AI development tools.

The prototype focused on the field inspector workflow, helping users manage assigned jobs, capture inspection evidence, track checklist progress, and review issues before submission. The goal was to show product thinking beyond static wireframes by creating a functional mobile experience that could communicate workflow, interaction patterns, and user value in a realistic way.

Role
Product thinking, workflow definition, prototype direction, and AI-assisted build process
Tools
Rork, Expo Go, Claude Code
Outcome
Polished mobile prototype with assigned jobs, checklists, capture, review, validation, and submission readiness

Problem

A static concept could not show the field workflow in motion.

A static presentation or wireframe can explain an idea, but it often fails to show how a field workflow actually feels in motion. For this interview exercise, I wanted to demonstrate how a property inspection concept could become a practical mobile experience that supported the inspector’s real job to be done: know what inspections are assigned, understand what needs to be captured, complete required checklist items, and submit a clean review.

My Role

Product thinking translated into a working prototype.

I led the product thinking, workflow definition, prototype direction, and AI-assisted build process. I focused on translating the inspection workflow into a mobile experience that could be reviewed, tested, and discussed like a real app rather than a static concept.

Prototype Experience

The core field inspector workflow.

Today’s Jobs

Inspectors can see assigned inspections, job status, inspection type, distance, and progress.

Job Detail

Inspectors can open a job detail screen with property information, policyholder details, special notes, progress tracking, and a required checklist.

Capture

The capture flow supports taking photos tied to checklist items, showing item progress, photo requirements, and completion percentage.

Review & Submit

The review flow summarizes completed and flagged checklist areas, estimated earnings, severity level, and submission readiness.

Validation Logic

The prototype prevents submission when required review items are incomplete, making the workflow feel closer to a real operational tool.

How I Used AI

AI accelerated the move from product concept to working prototype.

Rork

Used Rork to accelerate the initial mobile app structure and interface generation.

Expo Go

Used Expo Go to test the prototype on a real mobile device, making the experience easier to evaluate like an actual app instead of a static design.

Claude Code

Used Claude Code to support iterative refinement, cleanup, and workflow improvements.

Product review

Used the working prototype to evaluate workflow clarity, screen transitions, hierarchy, and whether the experience communicated the product concept effectively.

Process

From field job to mobile prototype.

  1. Started with the field inspector job to be done.
  2. Defined the core workflow from assigned jobs to job detail, capture, review, and submission.
  3. Built a mobile prototype instead of relying only on static wireframes.
  4. Used AI-assisted tools to accelerate interface generation and iteration.
  5. Tested the experience through Expo Go on a real device.
  6. Refined the workflow to make progress, required items, issue states, and submission readiness clear.

Outcome

A tangible mobile workflow for discussion and review.

The result was a polished mobile prototype that made the inspection workflow tangible. It showed how an inspector could manage daily assignments, understand inspection requirements, capture evidence, track progress, review issues, and prepare a submission. The project demonstrated rapid prototyping, product judgment, mobile workflow design, and practical use of AI tools in an interview setting.

What I Learned

Working prototypes make product thinking easier to evaluate.

This project reinforced the value of building something reviewable when the goal is to communicate product thinking. A working prototype makes tradeoffs, workflow gaps, and user value easier to discuss. AI helped accelerate execution, but the product value came from defining the workflow, focusing on the user’s job, and turning the concept into something tangible.
