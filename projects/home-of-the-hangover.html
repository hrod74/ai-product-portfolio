Home of the Hangover Website Rebuild

Rebuilding a 10-year-old bottle shop website into a modern, mobile-first customer experience with AI-assisted product strategy, QA, and development workflows.

Home of the Hangover is a craft beer, liquor, wine, cigar, and specialty bottle shop in Big Bear Lake, California. I originally built the store’s first website in 2016 using a purchased template and manual customization. Nearly a decade later, the owners came back ready for a modern refresh. Using today’s AI tools, I rebuilt the site from scratch into a faster, cleaner, mobile-first experience with clearer positioning, improved customer actions, direct GA4 tracking, and a refreshed brand presence.

Before the Rebuild

Before the Rebuild

The original Home of the Hangover website was built years earlier from a purchased template. It helped the business get online, but over time it became dated, harder to maintain, and less aligned with how customers browse on mobile today.

The rebuild started by looking at what the old site did well, what needed to be preserved, and where the experience needed to improve.

Problem

Modernizing a site that no longer matched the business.

The original site had not been meaningfully updated in years. It was based on an older template, harder to maintain, and did not reflect the store’s current positioning, selection, or customer needs. The owners needed a modern site that made it easy for customers to call, get directions, check hours, understand delivery, and see why the store is known for its curated mountain selection.

Background Story

A second build, almost a decade later.

Joe and Ashley own Home of the Hangover. Joe and I have been friends for a long time, and when they first bought the store, he asked me to help create a website for the business. At the time, I had a full-time job, so I bought a website template and customized it over roughly two months to fit the store’s needs.

Years later, Joe came back and asked about upgrading the website. This time, the available AI tools changed the entire workflow. Instead of starting from a template, I rebuilt the site from scratch with AI-assisted development, modern responsive design, cleaner content, easier maintenance, and analytics that could help show the value of customer actions like calls, directions, delivery clicks, and email.

My Role

End-to-end product direction and delivery support.

I led the rebuild end to end, including product direction, content strategy, AI workflow design, AI-assisted development, visual QA, analytics setup, deployment support, and iteration with the owners. I also created a refreshed logo concept using Midjourney to help modernize the brand presence.

How I Used AI

AI supported the full product lifecycle, not just code generation.

Site audit

Used AI to audit the existing website, review content gaps, identify outdated structure, and evaluate the current hosting and deployment setup.

Strategy and planning

Built complete strategy and planning docs to define the site goals, customer actions, section structure, content priorities, tracking needs, and launch approach.

Workflow design

Created custom AI workflows to support build planning, content iteration, implementation review, QA, release notes, and deployment from local development to production.

Custom QA support

Built custom skills and repeatable prompts to QA the site across desktop and mobile, check copy consistency, review analytics events, and catch deployment issues.

Build and iteration

Used Claude Code, Cursor, and ChatGPT to accelerate the rebuild, while keeping product judgment, owner feedback, and manual QA at the center of the process.

Brand exploration

Used Midjourney to create a refreshed logo direction that better matched the store’s personality and Big Bear mountain setting.

Process

From audit to launch, with AI speeding up the work around human decisions.

  1. Audited the existing site, hosting setup, customer journey, and content structure.
  2. Clarified the store’s positioning around craft beer, liquor, wine, cigars, delivery, kegs, and curated mountain finds.
  3. Created strategy and planning docs before rebuilding the site.
  4. Used AI tools to accelerate copy exploration, layout planning, implementation, and QA.
  5. Rebuilt the site as a clean, static, mobile-first website.
  6. Created and refined a new logo concept with Midjourney.
  7. Added direct GA4 tracking for key customer actions.
  8. Used custom QA workflows to review desktop, mobile, copy, analytics, and deployment readiness.
  9. Iterated based on owner feedback and real-world deployment checks.
  10. Deployed from local development to production on GoDaddy hosting.

Outcome

A cleaner customer experience with better action visibility.

The rebuilt site launched as a faster, more modern, easier-to-maintain customer experience. The owners approved the new direction, the site now better reflects the store’s personality and selection, and GA4 click tracking provides visibility into customer actions such as calls, directions, delivery interest, social clicks, and email.

What I Learned

AI is most useful when paired with judgment and QA.

This project showed how much faster practical AI workflows can make a small-business website rebuild when paired with product judgment, customer focus, and hands-on QA. AI helped compress the audit, planning, build, and QA cycles, but the important decisions still came from understanding the business, the customer journey, and what the owners actually needed.
